Agile Methods

The 12 principles of the Agile Manifesto

  1. Our highest priority is to satisfy the customer through early and continuous delivery of valuable software.
  2. Welcome changing requirements, even late in development. Agile processes harness change for the customer's competitive advantage.
  3. Deliver working software frequently, from a couple of weeks to a couple of months, with a preference to the shorter timescale.
  4. Business people and developers must work together daily throughout the project.
  5. Build projects around motivated individuals. Give them the environment and support they need, and trust them to get the job done.
  6. The most efficient and effective method of conveying information to and within a development team is face-to-face conversation.
  7. Working software is the primary measure of progress.
  8. Agile processes promote sustainable development. The sponsors, developers, and users should be able to maintain a constant pace indefinitely.
  9. Continuous attention to technical excellence and good design enhances agility.
  10. Simplicity--the art of maximizing the amount of work not done--is essential.
  11. The best architectures, requirements, and designs emerge from self-organizing teams.
  12. At regular intervals, the team reflects on how to become more effective, then tunes and adjusts its behavior accordingly.

What does working Agile mean?

Working agile is something that has been becoming more popular over the recent years. And working agile means you follow a set of principles and methods within program development and project management. These principles and methods aim to increase flexibility, cooperation and keeping a focus on customer needs. Agile methods value the individuals and interactions in front of proccesses and tools, working software in front of extensive documentation, customer cooperation in front of contract negotation and adjustement to change in front of following a plan. This doesn't mean you don't value the other points but you put an emphasis on the ones mentioned.

How do you work agile?

When working agile there are many different approaches. There are methods such as Scrum, Kanban, Extreme Programming(XP) and many more. Depending on your context and needs you will have to pick the most suitable method, or you can combine several of them to tailor to your needs. To work agile there are a few key things your must do in order to prepare. I will describe this process as how it can be used using SCRUM as the method of agile work.

Scrum Preparation

Firstly you need to gather a SCRUM team. This team will be composed of a SCRUM master who oversees the workflow and makes sure you stay aligned to the method. There will also be the productowner who maintains contact with the client and align your goals with the client needs. He will also be the one keeping in touch with the client. And lastly you'll have the product developers, who are the actualy employees developing the solution for the client.

Secondly you'll need to start planning what needs to be done, you'll oversee the clients needs and what they want the final product to be like. Using this information you'll create a product backlog which will contain everything that needs to be done in order for the project to be finished. You'll also need to prioritise these tasks after what's most essential to the client and getting a working product.

Scrum Workflow

At the start of the scrum cycle you walk through the Product Backlog and put the most important things to the product at the top of the list to be done, you'll also be assigning the different tasks estimated timepoints (which is how long you estimate it'll take to complete that specific task). You'll be working in shorts sprints with your team about 2-4 weeks long and in preparation for these sprints you'll transfer the most important tasks from the product backlog to the Sprint backlog. Planning the sprint you'll be assigning the different tasks to your teammembers based on previous experience of tasks with the same background.

Then the team will go forward with the sprint and work with the different tasks assigned to them. There'll be daily meetings to keep the team synchronized and talk through any difficulties or problems that arises in the current tasks. During these meetings the teammembers will answer the following questions: "What did i do yesterday", "What am i doing today" and "Are there any obstacles".

After every sprint is finished there will be a sprint review where the team presents the work they've done for the productowner and others involved that'll give feedback. After that it'll be decided if it was a successful sprint or not. There'll also be a sprint retrospective where the team reflects over what went well and the cooperation during the sprint. The goal being to define what can be done better in the next sprint. After those tasks are done the productowner updates the backlog. Removing tasks that where done, adding new ones that appeared during the sprint and changing priorities based on needs.

Conclusively agile methods are a way to streamline a process and effectivise it, bringing important factors into the process such as feedback from the product owner and getting a usable product as soon as possible, which can be continously improved upon. The methods are also used to analyse and improve upon how the team works together.
